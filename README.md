**Comprehensive Study Plan for 2025-2027**

---

**Overview:**
This plan is designed to cover the first-year courses typically offered in Physics, Mathematics, Literature, Sociology, and Philosophy at top universities. The courses are distributed across quarters, with three courses per quarter, allowing for a 2-week break after each quarter.

**Quarterly Course Schedule:**

**Q1 2025 (Jan-Mar):**

1. **Classical Mechanics (Physics)**

   - *Topics:* Newtonian mechanics, motion in one and two dimensions, energy, momentum, and rotational dynamics.
   - *Goals:* Develop a solid foundation in the principles governing physical motion.
   - *Resources:* [MIT OpenCourseWare: 8.01SC Classical Mechanics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/8-01sc-classical-mechanics-fall-2016/), [Yale Open Yale Courses: PHYS 200 - Fundamentals of Physics I](https://oyc.yale.edu/physics/phys-200), [Stanford: Classical Mechanics by Prof. Leonard Susskind](https://theoreticalminimum.com/courses/classical-mechanics/2011/fall)

2. **Calculus I (Mathematics)**

   - *Topics:* Limits, derivatives, applications of differentiation, and introduction to integrals.
   - *Goals:* Build mathematical tools essential for physics and engineering.
   - *Resources:* [MIT OpenCourseWare: 18.01SC Single Variable Calculus](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-01sc-single-variable-calculus-fall-2010/), [Khan Academy: Calculus 1](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/calculus-1), [Professor Leonard's Calculus 1 Full Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLF797E961509B4EB5), [UNC Chapel Hill: Calculus 1 - Full College Course](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HfACrKJ_Y2w)

3. **Introduction to Literary Studies (Literature)**

   - *Topics:* Literary analysis, close reading techniques, and understanding genres.
   - *Goals:* Cultivate skills to critically analyze literary texts.
   - *Resources:* [Yale University: Introduction to Theory of Literature](https://online.yale.edu/courses/introduction-theory-literature), [University of Tübingen: Lecture Course: Introduction to Literary Studies](https://timms.uni-tuebingen.de/tp/UT_20230126_001_ws2223litstud_0001), [NPTEL: Introduction to Literary Theory](https://archive.nptel.ac.in/courses/109/104/109104135/), [Harvard University: Masterpieces of World Literature](https://www.harvardonline.harvard.edu/course/masterpieces-world-literature)

**Break:** Apr (2 weeks)

---

**Q2 2025 (Apr-Jun):**

1. **Electromagnetism (Physics)**

   - *Topics:* Electric fields, magnetic fields, circuits, and Maxwell’s equations.
   - *Goals:* Understand the fundamental principles of electricity and magnetism.
   - *Resources:* [MIT OpenCourseWare: Physics II: Electricity and Magnetism](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/8-02-physics-ii-electricity-and-magnetism-spring-2019/), [Coursera: Introduction to Electricity and Magnetism Specialization](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/introduction-to-electricity-magnetism), [Dr. Andrew Mitchell's Electromagnetism Lecture Series](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLotxEOxVaaoLMvrDSSr_nsQDiKcVu8d7u)

2. **Calculus II (Mathematics)**

   - *Topics:* Integration techniques, applications of integrals, and infinite series.
   - *Goals:* Expand mathematical proficiency for advanced problem-solving.
   - *Resources:* [Professor Leonard's Calculus II Full Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLDesaqWTN6EQ2J4vgsN1HyBeRADEh4Cw-), [Dr. Valerie Hower's Calculus II Full Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpcwHaLYiaEWLlLEdH2gpK2J7gTAXzB5N), [University of Utah's MATH 1220: Calculus II](https://www.math.utah.edu/lectures/math1220.php), [Calculus II Video Lectures by UMass Lowell](https://faculty.uml.edu/Jennifer_GonzalezZugasti/Calculus%20II%20Video%20Lectures/Calculus%20II%20Video%20Lectures.aspx)

3. **Survey of English Literature I (Literature)**

   - *Topics:* Major works and authors from early periods to the 18th century.
   - *Goals:* Gain historical context and appreciate foundational literary works.
   - *Resources:* [History of English Language and Literature (IIT Madras)](https://www.classcentral.com/course/swayam-history-of-english-language-and-literature-14108), [American Passages: A Literary Survey](https://www.learner.org/series/american-passages-a-literary-survey/), [Introduction to Theory of Literature (Yale)](https://oyc.yale.edu/english/engl-300)

**Break:** Jul (2 weeks)

---

**Q3 2025 (Jul-Sep):**

1. **Linear Algebra (Mathematics)**

   - *Topics:* Matrices, determinants, vector spaces, and eigenvalues.
   - *Goals:* Develop skills to solve systems of equations and analyze linear transformations.
   - *Resources:* [Dr. Trefor Bazett's Linear Algebra Full Course](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLHXZ9OQGMqxfUl0tcqPNTJsb7R6BqSLo6), [Khan Academy: Linear Algebra](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/linear-algebra), [Gilbert Strang's Linear Algebra Lectures on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL49CF3715CB9EF31D), [MIT OpenCourseWare: 18.06 Linear Algebra](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-06-linear-algebra-spring-2010/)

2. **Discrete Mathematics (Mathematics)**

   - *Topics:* Logic, set theory, combinatorics, and graph theory.
   - *Goals:* Learn mathematical reasoning and problem-solving techniques.
   - *Resources:* [Discrete Math I by Kimberly Brehm](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLl-gb0E4MII28GykmtuBXNUNoej-vY5Rz), [Discrete Math by Dr. Trefor Bazett](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLHXZ9OQGMqxersk8fUxiUMSIx0DBqsKZS), [Discrete Mathematics Full Course on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UwYJUKVc-Hs), [Mathematics for Computer Science (MIT OCW)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-042j-mathematics-for-computer-science-fall-2010/), [Discrete Mathematics (Shanghai Jiao Tong University - Coursera)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/discrete-mathematics), [Wolfram U: Introduction to Discrete Mathematics](https://www.wolfram.com/wolfram-u/courses/mathematics/introduction-to-discrete-mathematics/)

3. **Academic Writing and Composition (Literature)**

   - *Topics:* Essay structure, argumentative writing, and rhetorical analysis.
   - *Goals:* Enhance academic communication skills.
   - *Resources:* [English Composition I (Duke University)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/english-composition), [Academic English: Writing Specialization (UC Irvine)](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/academic-english), [Writing in English at University (Lund University)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/writing-english-university), [Writing in the Sciences (Stanford University)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/writing-in-the-sciences), [College Writing 101 (Udemy)](https://www.udemy.com/course/college-writing-success/)

**Break:** Oct (2 weeks)

---

**Q4 2025 (Oct-Dec):**

1. **Probability and Statistics (Mathematics)**

   - *Topics:* Basic probability, statistical methods, and data analysis.
   - *Goals:* Understand and apply statistical reasoning.
   - *Resources:* [MIT OpenCourseWare: Introduction to Probability and Statistics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-05-introduction-to-probability-and-statistics-spring-2022/), [Stat 110: Probability (Harvard University)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL2SOU6wwxB0uwwH80KTQ6ht66KWxbzTIo), [Introduction to Probability (Harvard edX)](https://pll.harvard.edu/course/introduction-probability-edx), [MIT OpenCourseWare: Probabilistic Systems Analysis](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-041-probabilistic-systems-analysis-and-applied-probability-fall-2010/video_galleries/video-lectures/), [Wolfram U: Introduction to Probability](https://www.wolfram.com/wolfram-u/courses/mathematics/introduction-to-probability/)

2. **Introduction to Abstract Algebra (Mathematics)**

   - *Topics:* Groups, rings, fields, and algebraic structures.
   - *Goals:* Gain foundational knowledge for higher-level mathematics.
   - *Resources:* [Modern Algebra (MIT OpenCourseWare)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-703-modern-algebra-spring-2013/), [Introduction to Abstract and Linear Algebra (NPTEL)](https://onlinecourses.nptel.ac.in/noc24_ma58/preview), [Introduction to Abstract Algebra: Group Theory (Udemy)](https://www.udemy.com/course/abstract-algebra-a-crash-course-in-group-theory/), [Honors Abstract Algebra (Harvard University)](https://people.math.harvard.edu/~ctm/home/text/class/harvard/55a/08/html/home/course/course.pdf)

3. **Survey of English Literature II (Literature)**

   - *Topics:* Romanticism, Victorian literature, and early 20th-century works.
   - *Goals:* Continue building historical and critical understanding of literature.

**Break:** Jan 2026 (2 weeks)

---

**Q1 2026 (Jan-Mar):**

1. **Introduction to Sociology (Sociology)**

   - *Topics:* Key sociological theories, social structures, and cultural dynamics.
   - *Goals:* Understand the principles governing human societies.
   - *Resources:* [Foundations of Modern Social Theory (Yale University)](https://oyc.yale.edu/sociology/socy-151/lecture-1), [Introduction to Sociology (Alison)](https://alison.com/course/introduction-to-sociology), [Introduction to Sociology (NPTEL)](https://www.nptelvideos.com/course.php?id=634), [Introduction to Sociology Lectures (YouTube Playlist)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLtMRjzg3I_PURIs_sqg4Dj_CWeHcj0ydF)

2. **Social Problems (Sociology)**

   - *Topics:* Contemporary societal issues such as inequality, crime, and health.
   - *Goals:* Analyze real-world problems through a sociological lens.
   - *Resources:* [Social Problems: Who Makes Them? (The Open University)](https://www.open.edu/openlearn/society-politics-law/sociology/social-problems-who-makes-them/content-section-0), [Understanding and Solving Poverty and Inequality (FutureLearn)](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/understanding-and-solving-social-problems), [Sociology: Social Issues and Change (Alison)](https://alison.com/course/sociology-social-issues-and-change), [Solving Public Problems (NYU GovLab)](https://blog.thegovlab.org/post/this-free-online-class-will-teach-anyone-how-to-solve-societal-problems), [Social Psychology (UC Berkeley)](https://freevideolectures.com/course/3591/social-psychology)

3. **Research Methods in Sociology (Sociology)**

   - *Topics:* Quantitative and qualitative research, survey design, and data collection.
   - *Goals:* Learn to conduct sociological research.
   - *Resources:* [Understanding Research Methods (SOAS University of London)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/research-methods), [Qualitative Research Methods (University of Amsterdam)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/qualitative-methods), [Methods and Statistics in Social Sciences Specialization (University of Amsterdam)](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/social-science), [Introduction to Social Research Methods (The University of Edinburgh)](https://www.edx.org/learn/research/the-university-of-edinburgh-introduction-to-social-research-methods)

**Break:** Apr (2 weeks)

---

**Q2 2026 (Apr-Jun):**

1. **Sociological Theory (Sociology)**

   - *Topics:* Classical and contemporary sociological theories.
   - *Goals:* Understand key theoretical frameworks.
   - *Resources:* [Foundations of Modern Social Theory (Yale University)](https://oyc.yale.edu/sociology/socy-151), [Classical Sociological Theory (University of Amsterdam)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/classical-sociological-theory), [Sociological Theory (Yale University YouTube Playlist)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL37FC9556148B7E1F)

2. **Cultural Anthropology (Sociology)**

   - *Topics:* Human cultures, traditions, and practices across societies.
   - *Goals:* Develop a comparative perspective on cultural diversity.
   - *Resources:* [ANTH101: Anthropology for Everyone](https://anth101.com/), [Diploma in Cultural Anthropology (Alison)](https://alison.com/course/diploma-in-cultural-anthropology), [Introduction to Anthropology (MIT OpenCourseWare)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/21a-00-introduction-to-anthropology-spring-2022/)

3. **Statistics for Social Sciences (Sociology)**

   - *Topics:* Data analysis and interpretation for social science research.
   - *Goals:* Enhance skills in statistical application.
   - *Resources:* [Methods and Statistics in Social Sciences Specialization (University of Amsterdam)](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/social-science), [Data Analysis for Social Scientists (MIT OpenCourseWare)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/14-310x-data-analysis-for-social-scientists-spring-2023/), [Basic Statistics (University of Amsterdam)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/basic-statistics), [Statistics and Probability (Khan Academy)](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/statistics-probability)

**Break:** Jul (2 weeks)

---

**Q3 2026 (Jul-Sep):**

1. **Introduction to Philosophy (Philosophy)**

   - *Topics:* Key philosophical questions and methods of inquiry.
   - *Goals:* Develop critical thinking and reasoning skills.
   - *Resources:* [Introduction to Philosophy (University of Edinburgh)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/philosophy), [General Philosophy (University of Oxford)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLKuMaHOvHA4pl2k1JNikMzccot_Y_vlYm), [Introduction to Political Philosophy (Yale University)](https://oyc.yale.edu/political-science/plsc-114), [Introduction to Philosophy: God, Knowledge and Consciousness (MITx)](https://mitxonline.mit.edu/courses/course-v1:MITxT+24.00x/)

2. **Ethics (Philosophy)**

   - *Topics:* Moral theories, ethical dilemmas, and decision-making frameworks.
   - *Goals:* Explore the foundations of ethical reasoning.

3. **Logic (Philosophy)**

   - *Topics:* Propositional logic, argument analysis, and formal reasoning.
   - *Goals:* Learn to construct and evaluate arguments.

**Break:** Oct (2 weeks)

---

**Q4 2026 (Oct-Dec):**

1. **History of Ancient Philosophy (Philosophy)**

   - *Topics:* Pre-Socratic to Hellenistic philosophers.
   - *Goals:* Understand the origins of Western philosophy.

2. **Political Philosophy (Philosophy)**

   - *Topics:* Justice, liberty, and the role of the state.
   - *Goals:* Analyze political systems and ideas.

3. **Philosophy of Mind (Philosophy)**

   - *Topics:* Consciousness, identity, and the nature of thought.
   - *Goals:* Explore philosophical questions about the mind.

**Break:** Jan 2027 (2 weeks)

---

**Q1 2027 (Jan-Mar):**

1. **General Chemistry (Physics)**

   - *Topics:* Atomic structure, chemical bonding, and reactions.
   - *Goals:* Build foundational knowledge for physical sciences.

2. **Introduction to Computer Programming (Physics)**

   - *Topics:* Programming basics, algorithms, and applications in physics.
   - *Goals:* Gain computational skills for scientific analysis.

3. **World Literature (Literature)**

   - *Topics:* Major works and themes from global literary traditions.
   - *Goals:* Expand literary perspective beyond Western traditions.

**Break:** Apr (2 weeks)

---

**Goals Summary:**

- Develop strong foundational knowledge in five diverse fields.
- Build interdisciplinary skills in analysis, research, and critical thinking.
- Create a balanced and sustainable learning schedule.

**How to Use This Plan:**

- Print and display the quarterly schedule.
- Focus on weekly goals for each course.
- Celebrate milestones after each quarter to stay motivated!
